Verb Moods: Indicative and Imperative
The indicative mood is the most common verb mood in English. It is used to express facts and opinions,
or to ask questions about them. The indicative mood is used in all verb tenses.
The imperative mood is used to make requests or give orders and warnings. It is used only in the 
simple present and future tenses.I am going to visit Paris next week.
You looked happy yesterday.
My dog is barking at passing cars.
Does she have my book?
Do they study English on Tuesdays?
